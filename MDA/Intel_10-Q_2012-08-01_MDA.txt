Fair Value of Financial Instruments When determining fair value, we consider the principal or most advantageous market in which we would transact, and we consider assumptions that market participants would use when pricing the asset or liability. For further information, see Note 4: Fair Value in the Notes to Consolidated Condensed Financial Statements of this Form 10-Q. Credit risk is factored into the valuation of financial instruments that we measure and record at fair value. When fair value is determined using pricing models, such as a discounted cash flow model, the obligors credit risk is factored into the calculation of the fair value, as appropriate.
